# Sector-Specific Analysis

Examples of required context:

- Banks: ROE, NIM, credit loss, capital ratio.
- REITs: FFO, AFFO, occupancy, debt maturity.
- Biotech: pipeline, trial stage, cash runway, R&D burn.
- Transport: yield, utilization, fuel, fleet, orderbook.
- Retail: same-store sales, traffic, inventory, store count.
- Utilities: rate base, allowed ROE, dividend coverage.
- Space/aerospace small caps: launch or mission services, contract backlog, customer concentration, execution milestones, cash burn, financing need.
- A-share companies: use Chinese reporting terms and RMB units; do not describe them as SEC filing businesses.

## A-share Sector Checklists

Battery / new energy manufacturing:

- Start with what the company sells: EV batteries, energy-storage batteries, battery materials, modules, or systems.
- Check revenue growth against gross margin, capex, capacity utilization, inventory, receivables, and customer concentration.
- Treat R&D and capex as operating questions, not decorative line items.
- Do not analyze battery manufacturers as banks, insurers, software platforms, or ordinary consumer brands.

Insurance / integrated financials:

- Separate premium income, underwriting result, investment income, solvency, and capital adequacy where data is available.
- Watch asset-liability mismatch, investment yield pressure, reserve adequacy, and life/property insurance mix.
- Do not use industrial FCF, net debt / EBITDA, or consumer-brand language as the core frame.

Pharma / innovative drug:

- Separate approved-drug portfolio revenue, R&D intensity, pipeline/approval progress, reimbursement pressure, and patent/competition risk.
- Do not reduce an established pharma company to an early-biotech cash-runway-only story.
- Flag missing product, indication, approval, and reimbursement data as data gaps.

Mining / nonferrous metals:

- Identify the commodity exposure first: gold, copper, other metals, smelting, or mine development.
- Check production volume, commodity price cycle, operating cash flow, capex, debt, reserve life, FX, and jurisdiction risk.
- Do not analyze resource producers as biotech, software, banks, or insurance companies.
