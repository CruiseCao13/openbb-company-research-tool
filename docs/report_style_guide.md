# Report Style Guide

Reports should read like first-pass research memos, not terminal logs.

## Structure

Every report should include:

- title and generated metadata
- table of contents
- report status card
- one-line verdict
- asset profile
- thesis spine
- AI analyst red flags
- research battle card
- key questions and answers
- chart walkthrough
- business quality
- risk and resilience
- valuation stress test
- data audit and methodology
- next research steps
- metric definitions and data deficit appendix

## Language

English reports should be English. Chinese reports should be Chinese.

Chinese reports should use natural Chinese investment-research prose. Do not translate English section labels directly when a better Chinese expression exists.

## Charts

Every chart needs:

- figure number or clear title
- readable file path
- explanation of what the chart shows
- interpretation of what the report reads from it
- warning about how not to misread it
- what to check next when useful

## Tables

Every major table needs:

- a short intro
- units or obvious metric formatting
- a short interpretation after the table

Do not leave bare tables as the main research experience.
